Modern Art

Modern art is a style of art that arose in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. It was distinguished by a departure from established styles in favor of a more abstract, experimental approach to making works of art. Impressionism, Expressionism, Cubism, Fauvism, Dadaism, and Surrealism are examples of major modern art trends. Pablo Picasso, Wassily Kandinsky, Salvador Dal, and Marcel Duchamp were all influential modernist artists. Modern art has had a long-lasting influence on the e volution of visual culture and continues to do so now.


Some of the artwork created during this time period was in more traditional or classical styles. However, the word "modern art" is connected with art that defied previous conventions and approaches.

Impression, Sunrise (Impressionism)

credited with coining the word "Impressionism." Monet made six separate paintings of his hometown's harbor in Le Havre, France. The most well-known of the series is Impression Sunrise, which was presented alongside works by other painters in 1874, when this new kind of painting garnered a lot of criticism.
Completed in 1872, this painting depicts a seascape scene from Le Havre, France, where Monet is said to have spent a significant amount of time throughout his life. The painting is notable for its delicate, but seductive hues, which depict a peaceful daybreak over the busy port city at the time Monet painted it. The larger ships and barges are hardly visible as they disappear into the early morning mist.

Drowning Girl (American Pop)

American pop artist Roy Lichtenstein created one of the most unforgettable paintings of the 1960s with his famous Drowning Girl. This piece was inspired by Secret Love #83, a classic DC Comics book that depicted a dramatic scene in which the heroine drowns recklessly in front of her boyfriend. Lichtenstein portrayed his joy in this painting by focusing on the profoundly evocative but distant landscape of grief. The girl's precarious situation is emphasized by swirling water enveloping her head as she falls beneath the waves.
